Introduction

The Culture Department of Sindh, established as a "Culture Cell" in 1976 and elevated to an Administrative Department in 1988, aims to preserve and promote the province's cultural heritage. Initially covering multiple areas, it was eventually divided into separate Culture and Tourism Departments in 2009.

In 2010, the department took over 126 archaeological sites and monuments, with more transferred in 2011 following constitutional changes. Its responsibilities include promoting Sindh's cultural heritage, supporting literary activities through public libraries, preserving historical sites, and assisting local intellectuals.

The department manages cultural complexes, theatres, and arts councils, and regularly publishes books and organizes literary events. It plans to expand its library network across the province and is actively seeking funding for archaeological preservation efforts. Additionally, it protects heritage buildings under the Sindh Cultural Heritage (Preservation) Act, 1994, and supports local writers and artists through various financial aid initiatives.
